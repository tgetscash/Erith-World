## BACKSTORY

### Origins
Cassian's father is **Lord Aldric Solvar** — a senior figure in a Valdris noble house with deep Aureate Compact affiliations, a documented four-circle mage from a bloodline producing competent mages for six generations. Not a headline name but a significant one — the kind of person whose decisions quietly matter in Compact internal politics.

His mother — **Lira Navitas** — is listed in official records as a household servant. Her death record from Cassian's birth is classified under a Compact security designation that shouldn't apply to a servant's death. The attending medic's notes were sealed within 48 hours. Two servants who assisted at the birth were relocated to different estates within the month. What actually happened during Cassian's birth, only a very small circle knows. Whatever it was, Lord Solvar had enough information within hours to begin erasing the record. The classification wasn't to protect the family's reputation. It was to hide evidence of something.

Cassian knows none of this. He knows his mother's name because it was on the single document he was handed when he was cast out. He knows nothing else about her.

### Early Life
The first years of his life were spent in the Solvar estate — not as an acknowledged son but as a ward. Tolerated rather than welcomed. Early cultivation training was pragmatic rather than paternal — noble houses don't waste a potential cultivator regardless of parentage. Tutors assessed both paths, trained him in fundamentals of magic theory and physical cultivation basics, and waited to see where his aptitude pointed.

It pointed nowhere. Meridian assessments came back abnormal. Treatment was attempted — three different methods, including an expensive Saal Conclave-licensed procedure. None worked. The tutors' conclusion: permanently clogged, no cultivation potential, not a viable investment.

Lord Solvar had him removed from the estate within the week. Cassian was nine years old. He was handed a document with his mother's name on it and enough Marks to last a month.

He took her surname.

### Life in Velmoor
The years between nine and seventeen are not a heroic survival story. They are just hard. Velmoor absorbed him the way industrial cities absorb people with nowhere else to go — into the working machinery of it. Magitech repair shops, loading work, eventually a semi-permanent arrangement with an elderly Keldi engineer named **Osset** who recognised that the boy who kept fixing things that weren't his to fix might be useful. Osset is not warm exactly but he is fair, which at nine felt like extraordinary kindness and at seventeen feels like the closest thing to stable ground Cassian has.

He taught himself cultivation theory from salvaged texts and borrowed books during the years he couldn't sleep. Not because he thought he'd use it — the meridians weren't going to un-clog through force of will. Because understanding the thing he couldn't have felt important. Because he is constitutionally incapable of not understanding how something works.

### The Idea
The idea came slowly. Not a revelation — more like a conclusion he kept reaching from different directions, kept dismissing, and kept coming back to. The meridians can't connect to a circle anchor. Can't connect to the heart for aura. What if that wasn't the whole problem? What if that was actually a different kind of question entirely?

He has been working out the theory for two years. Three notebooks worth of cultivation theory cross-referenced against everything he's read about meridian mechanics and what the body actually does with mana. He thinks he understands the mechanism. He doesn't know if his body can survive it. He is seventeen and increasingly convinced he is going to find out anyway.

## SPECIAL PHYSIQUE — THE ETHEREAL MARROW

**Classification:** Exceptional tier. No documented equivalent in Saal Conclave public records. Possibly described in Solen Ess's unpublished 30%.

**What it is:** Cassian's meridians are not purely biological tissue. Distributed through their structure are microscopic deposits of crystallised Cosmic Mana — present from birth, invisible to standard meridian assessment, only detectable by the most advanced diagnostic techniques the Thalaran Meridian Institute possesses. Every examination Cassian has ever had returned simply: clogged.

**What it means:**
His meridians can passively absorb all three mana types — Cosmic, Living, and Dying — rather than just Living Mana like every other cultivator on earth. The crystallised Cosmic deposits act as conversion points, processing ambient mana from all sources simultaneously. He has been doing this since birth without knowing it.

The clog is not a blockage in the conventional sense. It is the meridian system's failure to route the absorbed mana anywhere — no heart connection, no circle anchor. The mana has nowhere to go. So it accumulates. Eight years of continuous tri-mana absorption stored in a physique built to contain it. The crystalline meridian structure is why it hasn't killed him — ordinary meridians would have ruptured years ago under this pressure.

**Why standard treatment failed:** The Saal Conclave's licensed treatment procedures assume standard Living Mana meridians. Cassian's meridians are partially crystalline Cosmic Mana structures. The treatment wasn't addressing what was actually there.

**The implication:** When cleansed — if cleansed — the release will not be a normal meridian opening. It will be eight years of compressed, unconverted, tri-source mana suddenly requiring direction. If he has somewhere to direct it. If he survives directing it. The Dantian formation requires exactly this kind of mana volume and quality — conventional cultivators attempting the path via destruction of their existing cultivation are working with single-source refined mana. Cassian has eight years of tri-source raw mana that has been passively purifying inside crystalline meridian structures. The difference in available formation material, if he can access it, is significant.

**Hidden effect (unknown to Cassian):** The accumulated mana pressure provides passive minor physical reinforcement — not cultivation, just pressure doing structural work. Contributes a hidden +1 to effective Physique that Cassian cannot account for and doesn't know exists.

**The Untethered's designation:** Their internal documents contain a theoretical description of a physique matching this profile. They call it the **First Vessel**. They have been searching for a confirmed case for sixty years.

## CULTIVATION PATH — THE DANTIAN

*This section tracks Cassian's personal cultivation path. Currently pre-activation.*

**Current Status:** Locked. Clogged meridians prevent all conventional cultivation. Mana accumulation ongoing passively — Cassian is unaware of this.

**The Theory (Cassian's current understanding):**
Instead of connecting meridians to a circle anchor (magic path) or to the heart (aura path), form a self-sustaining mana construct — a **Dantian** — from the mana itself. The Dantian acts as the central node the meridians feed into and draw from simultaneously. It doesn't commit to either path. The output is path-agnostic and should, theoretically, be able to produce effects resembling magic or aura depending on application — or eventually, something that resembles neither.

**Formation conditions as Cassian currently understands them:**
The formation requires a significant mana volume — more than standard mana, different in quality from normal cultivation energy. The body has to have somewhere to route that mana precisely and immediately during the formation attempt. The will to hold the forming construct in place under extreme duress while the body is simultaneously under stress it has never experienced. Failure modes: the construct collapses, the released mana has nowhere to go, the body fails to contain it. Probable outcome of failure: death.

**Why Cassian believes his specific situation creates a viable window:**
His clogged meridians have been accumulating mana for eight years. His physique has been containing it without rupture — which tells him his body can handle the pressure. The mana is already there. He doesn't need to destroy an existing cultivation system to generate the formation volume. He needs to cleanse the meridians — which will release the accumulated mana — and simultaneously attempt to form the Dantian before that energy dissipates or tears him apart. One specific, brief window. He will either form it or he won't.

**Progression axes (post-formation):**

| **Stability** | Purity and refinement of held mana | TBD on formation |
