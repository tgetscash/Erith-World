# ERITH — WORLD DOCUMENT
### A Complete Reference for the RPG Setting
*As established January 2020*

---

> **Usage Note:** This document covers the world of Erith in full. It is intentionally separated from any individual character. All lore, systems, factions, and figures described here exist independently of any campaign or player character.

---

# PART ONE: THE WORLD

---

## 1.1 — OVERVIEW

**The world is called Erith.** Its geography is identical to Earth — same continents, same oceans, same tectonic structure. Everything human-made is fiction. Nations, peoples, histories, religions, and the cultivation systems that define this world are entirely original.

The calendar and timekeeping systems align with our own for convenience. The story begins **January 1st, 2020.**

All inhabitants speak a common tongue for gameplay convenience, though regional dialects and languages are implied.

**The defining feature of Erith** is the existence of two related but distinct cultivation systems — Magic and Aura — both rooted in the same biological infrastructure (meridians and acupoints) and the same fundamental energy (mana). These systems permeate every aspect of Erith's civilisation, from politics to economics to daily life.

---

## 1.2 — THE THREE MANA TYPES

Mana is the fundamental energy underlying all existence on Erith. It exists in three distinct states:

### COSMIC MANA
The background radiation of the universe. Emanates from stars, celestial bodies, and the cosmos itself. Present everywhere at low ambient levels. The raw source material from which all other mana derives. Living things passively absorb Cosmic Mana and convert it into Living Mana through biological processes — meridians are the organ that performs this conversion. Most people are entirely unaware this is happening.

### LIVING MANA
What cultivators use. Present in living things, in the earth, in the air. Generated constantly through geological and biological activity. Ley lines are concentrated rivers of Living Mana running along tectonic fault lines. This is the mana that magic circles store and that aura users refine through their bodies.

### DYING MANA
Released when living things die and meridian networks collapse. Under normal circumstances, Dying Mana dissipates rapidly back into Cosmic Mana — the natural cycle completing itself. The Ashen Covenant (see: Religions) believes this mana can be harvested and wielded. Most of mainstream cultivation culture considers this deeply taboo, partly because breaking this natural cycle feels fundamentally wrong at an instinctive level that precedes conscious thought.

---

## 1.3 — LEY LINES AND FAULT LINES

Ley lines are concentrations of Living Mana that form along tectonic fault lines. When tectonic plates move and fracture, Living Mana seeps from deep within the earth and concentrates along the fractures rather than dispersing evenly.

**The more active the fault, the denser the ley line.**

This principle shapes the entire geopolitical landscape of Erith:

- **The Ring of Fire** (Pacific coastline — Japan, Philippines, Indonesia, the Americas) represents the most mana-dense geography on earth by a significant margin.
- **The Himalayan collision zone** (Indian Plate driving under the Eurasian) produces deep, stable ley lines ideal for advanced circle formation.
- **The East African Rift** (running through what is called Vaskara) produces unusual mana signatures that interact with local mineral deposits in unique ways — producing Vakrite.
- **The Mediterranean fault systems** (African and Eurasian plate boundary) produce significant ley line activity across the Sorenne region.
- **Antarctica** sits on its own stable craton with almost no tectonic activity — the lowest ambient mana density on earth. Paradoxically ideal for certain kinds of pure mana research.

Cities that grew up near ley line convergence points became disproportionately powerful over time, regardless of other geographic advantages. The key cities section details the most significant of these.

---

## 1.4 — SPACE AND LOST KNOWLEDGE

### Ancient Space Travel
Fragmentary evidence — primarily pre-Circle Wars texts and the accounts of Verath the Grey — suggests that cultivators in the ancient world achieved something no modern cultivator can replicate: reaching space through cultivation alone. The theoretical mechanism, reconstructed from fragments, involves using aura cultivation at an extreme stage to coat and protect the body against vacuum and temperature extremes while using high-circle magic to provide life support and propulsion.

Whether this was achieved by a single dual-path cultivator or a partnership working in perfect synchrony is unknown. The knowledge required was almost certainly lost during the Circle Wars when multiple centres of cultivation knowledge were deliberately or incidentally destroyed.

### The Moon
A persistent myth — present in multiple cultural traditions with no common source — holds that something was left on or reached the moon in the ancient period. Whether this is a relic, a record, or something else is entirely unknown. No expedition has reached the moon in recorded history. Current peak cultivation cannot achieve what the ancients apparently could.

### The Knowledge Gap
The Circle Wars (300-900) were so destructive that entire schools of cultivation knowledge were permanently lost. Several conflicts specifically targeted knowledge centres. The pre-collapse Karathi Empire likely possessed cultivation theory that hasn't been rediscovered. The possibility of space travel being achievable almost certainly resided in that lost knowledge.

This means **rediscovering lost knowledge is a live and consequential pursuit in 2020.** Ancient ruins matter. Old texts matter. A pre-Circle Wars archive, if one exists intact, would be a world-changing discovery.

# PART TWO: HISTORY

---

## ERA I — THE ANCIENT WORLD
*Pre-0 / Before the Circles — roughly 4000 BC to 0*

Civilisation rises across Erith much as it did on Earth. River valleys, agriculture, city-states, early empires. The meridian system exists in all humans but nobody knows it. Occasionally someone is born with uncanny endurance or unusual physical gifts — quietly attributed to gods or bloodline. These were almost certainly people with rare or clean meridians, but the knowledge didn't exist to explain it.

Three major ancient powers dominate this era and leave lasting cultural fingerprints:

**The Karathi Empire** (Africa/North Africa/Middle East equivalent) — The dominant civilisation of the ancient world. Agricultural, mercantile, theocratic. Their religion centres on *Saal* — an invisible current running through all living things. Closest ancient intuition about meridians. Karathi scholars will later be among the first to formalise magic theory.

**The Veltric Kingdoms** (Europe equivalent) — Loosely confederated city-states, warrior culture, constantly at war with each other. Produce the best soldiers of the ancient world, largely because their tribal selection pressures happened to favour people with cleaner meridian structures over generations — though nobody understood this at the time.

**The Dynari** (East/Southeast Asia equivalent) — The most philosophically sophisticated of the three. Already developing early concepts of breath, internal energy, balance. Their spiritual traditions will later map almost perfectly onto what meridians actually are, giving them a significant head start when aura cultivation becomes understood.

---

## ERA II — THE AWAKENING
*0 to 300*

The twin myth occurs around year 0. Two brothers — their names vary by culture and tradition, they may be mythological rather than historical — experience events that create the world's first mage and first aura user.

**The Myth:** One twin, whose meridians were clogged from birth, had a near-death experience that caused his meridians to detach from his heart. Bedridden and unknowingly meditating, he pulled in ambient mana over years, healing his meridians and forming his first magic circle — the circle connecting where the meridians now attached. He became the first mage.

His brother — whose meridians were unclogged — joined the army, mastering his body and weapons. When he was saved by his brother, the mage examined his meridians and cleansed them. The brother died in the process. But his meridians, now detached and cleansed, slowly drew in physical energy and reconnected to his heart. This was how the first aura user was born.

*Note: Whether this is historical fact or myth is academically contested. The Saal Conclave treats it as metaphorical truth. The Ironblood Brotherhood treats the aura twin as a literal ancestor. Most cultures have adapted the story to their own frameworks.*

Word spreads slowly. Within the first century it is mostly rumour and cult. By 150, the Karathi Empire institutionalises it — the first formal school of magic established, entry restricted to the priestly class. This creates the first Mage Aristocracy.

The Dynari develop aura cultivation almost independently, mapping it onto their existing internal philosophy. By 200 they have warrior monks meaningfully stronger than any conventional soldier.

By 300, every major civilisation knows magic and aura exist. The world will never be the same.

---

## ERA III — THE CIRCLE WARS
*300 to 900*

The era that shapes modern borders more than any other. When one person with three magic circles is worth a hundred soldiers, empires don't fall the way they used to — they get erased.

The Karathi Empire overextends trying to militarise its mage schools and collapses internally around 410. The vacuum it leaves fragments the Africa/Middle East equivalent into dozens of successor states. Some survive to 2020. Most don't.

The Veltric territories go through repeated cycles of unification under powerful aura warrior-kings followed by collapse when they die. No dynasty lasts more than four generations because heirs are almost never as gifted as founders. This pattern repeats six or seven times, leaving the Europe equivalent balkanised but tough.

The Dynari consolidate into two large powers — a northern empire built on aura warrior tradition and a southern one built on magic. They fight constantly; neither wins. This stalemate produces the most sophisticated understanding of both systems in the world by 900 because each side perpetually attempts to counter the other.

**Critical note:** The destruction of this era was catastrophic not just militarily but intellectually. Entire schools of cultivation knowledge were wiped out. Several conflicts specifically targeted knowledge centres. The pre-collapse Karathi Empire had cultivation theory — possibly including the knowledge required for space travel — that was lost and never recovered. We lost what we don't know we lost.

**Notable figure: ALDRIC VANE (~700 AD, Veltric)** — A Veltric warlord who came closer than anyone to unifying the European landmass. Not the most powerful aura user of his era but reputedly the most intelligent — the first recorded person to deliberately train soldiers in aura as a disciplined military doctrine rather than relying on naturally gifted individuals. His fragmented writings are still studied in every serious military institution on earth. Died at 61 by political assassination after never being defeated in battle. Used in the Veltric landmass the way Napoleon's name is used in France — complicated pride.

---

## ERA IV — CONSOLIDATION & THE OLD ORDERS
*900 to 1500*

Large stable nations form. Feudal structures dominate because a powerful mage or aura user at the top of a hierarchy is genuinely hard to displace. Noble houses consolidate around bloodlines showing consistent physique quality or magical aptitude over generations.

The most important development of this era isn't military — it's **the Orders.** Independent organisations transcending national borders, built around magic and aura mastery. Not governments, not religions, but institutions combining elements of medieval guild, knightly order, and university.

**The Aureate Compact** founded ~950 in the Veltric territories. Nominally neutral. Practically the most politically connected organisation in the western world. Certifies mage rankings, mediates disputes, funds research, operates schools. Headquarters in Valdris.

**The Ironblood Brotherhood** founded ~1100 in Dorai. Aura focused. Warrior tradition. Selective and brutal. Fiercely independent — refused absorption by every empire that tried.

**The Saal Conclave** — technically older, tracing roots to ~150, but formally established as an Order-equivalent institution around 950, separating from the Karathi state religion to become an independent body.

Notable figure of this era: **KARA THE UNBROKEN (~450 AD, Karathi)** — The last competent administrator of the Karathi Empire, holding it together for thirty years past when it should have collapsed. A six-circle mage whose legacy is primarily administrative rather than martial. Historians argue her tenure bought enough time for the Saal Conclave to properly establish itself. She destroyed her personal cultivation notes on her deathbed. The Conclave has never fully explained why they honoured that request.

---

## ERA V — THE MODERN SHAPING
*1500 to 2020*

Around 1500 an industrial-equivalent revolution begins — but goes differently. Magic exists, so certain technologies never develop (gunpowder weapons become obsolete almost immediately when a mid-tier mage can do more damage). Others accelerate: materials science, architecture, medicine, communication all advance rapidly with magical assistance.

**~1600: The Sorath Dominion** — A Karathi successor state briefly becomes the dominant world power, built on the old Saal philosophical tradition. They produce the first formal complete mapping of the meridian system in the human body. Their collapse around 1750 (internal succession crisis) scatters their scholars globally, effectively kickstarting a worldwide Renaissance of magical theory.

**~1900: The World Wars** — Not driven by nationalism and industrialism as in our history, but by Order politics and a disputed succession in the largest Veltric nation. Catastrophically destructive. Ends with the establishment of the **World Assembly** (international governance body) and the **Cultivator Accords** — the framework governing high-tier cultivator use in conflicts.

**2020:** Roughly 190 recognised nations. Most have parliamentary or presidential governments. Approximately 30 of those governments are genuinely independent of an old noble house or Order. The rest are managed democracies at best.

# PART THREE: NATIONS

---

## VELTRIC LANDMASS (Europe Equivalent)

### VALDRIS
*Location: Western Europe equivalent — Britain/France/Benelux*
**Capital: DUNEVORN** (Scotland/Highland area — near the Highland Boundary Fault and Southern Uplands Fault, two significant ley line convergences)
**Government:** Constitutional monarchy — Aureate Compact deeply entangled with the Crown
**Population:** ~180 million across mainland and island territories

The closest thing the western world has to a stable major power. King Edran III is himself a four-circle mage — unusually high for a sitting head of state. The noble houses of Valdris are old, politically vicious, and not going anywhere. A commoner can theoretically rise through the Compact or military — and occasionally does — but the ceiling is very visible.

Dunevorn grew around a natural ley line convergence. The original settlement was a cultivation monastery (~400 AD) that became a fortress, then a city, then a capital. Architecture reflects its layered history — ancient stone cultivation halls alongside modern infrastructure, the Aureate Compact's Auros Hall dominating the skyline.

*Notable for: Aureate Compact headquarters (Auros Hall). Best magical academia in the western world. Home of the World Assembly.*

---

### OSTERMARK
*Location: Central Europe equivalent — Germany/Austria/Poland*
**Capital:** Berlin equivalent (IRL name retained)
**Government:** Federal republic — genuinely more democratic than most neighbours

Emerged from the 1900s world-war equivalent having deliberately dismantled its old noble house structure. The Reform of 1919 theoretically stripped hereditary cultivator houses of legal authority. In practice, major houses rebranded as private institutions and corporations, maintaining influence through money rather than title.

Produces more registered aura users per capita than almost any other nation. Strong manufacturing and engineering base. One of the leading nations for magitech — this is where the Keldi engineering tradition and human industry intersect most productively.

*Notable for: Magitech industry. Ironblood Brotherhood administrative centre. The most genuine democracy in the Veltric landmass (which is relative).*

---

### SORENNE
*Location: Southern Europe equivalent — Italy/Spain/Southern France*
**Capital: SERANTHIS** (Southern Italy/Sicily area — where African and Eurasian plates meet, significant ley line convergence)
**Government:** Loose federation of city-states — functionally ruled by powerful mage families

A federation that exists mainly to present a unified face to the outside world. Culturally the most vibrant and arguably most corrupt nation in the western world. Three of the most powerful independent mage families on earth are based here, the Medori family most notably. The Medori have controlled Seranthis effectively for four centuries regardless of nominal governance.

Seranthis is the oldest continuously powerful city in the western world. When the Karathi Empire was at its height, Seranthis was already significant — the Mediterranean fault convergence was identified as a ley line node in pre-Circle Wars texts.

*Notable for: The Medori family — suspected of running the continent's underground Corestone trade. Seranthis's perpetually uninvestigated corruption. Beautiful, dangerous, ancient.*

---

### RUTHMARK
*Location: Eastern Europe/Russia equivalent*
**Capital:** Moscow equivalent (IRL name retained)
**Government:** Semi-authoritarian — governed by the Directorate, a council of seven high-tier cultivators whose identities are not publicly confirmed

Enormous. Spans from eastern Veltric landmass deep into northern Asia-equivalent border regions. The most militarised nation in the western world. Compulsory cultivation training from age twelve. The Directorate officially doesn't exist. Everyone knows it does.

The largest known concentration of rare physique births in the western world exists in northern Ruthmark — something in the northern regions produces unusual meridian configurations at a statistically anomalous rate. The Directorate quietly tracks every single one.

*Notable for: Largest cultivator military by volume in the western world. The Directorate. The anomalous rare physique birthrate in the north.*

---

## KARATHI LANDMASS (Africa/Middle East Equivalent)

### SORATH
*Location: North Africa/Egypt/Nile equivalent*
**Capital:** Cairo equivalent (IRL name retained)
**Government:** Constitutional republic — significantly undermined by the Saal Conclave

The most direct cultural descendant of the old Sorath Dominion and the Karathi Empire before it. Home to the Saal Conclave's headquarters, the Grand Archive of Karath, and the most sophisticated magical theory tradition on earth. The Conclave's mapping of the full meridian system in the 1700s is the foundation of modern cultivation medicine. They have never fully published it. They license it.

*Notable for: Grand Archive of Karath — largest collection of magical and aura theory texts in existence. Access tiered and expensive. Saal Conclave headquarters.*

---

### VASKARA
*Location: Sub-Saharan Africa equivalent*
**Capital: VARATH** (Ethiopia/Kenya Rift Valley area — East African Rift ley line convergence, directly above densest Vakrite deposits on earth)
**Government:** Federal presidential republic — Amara Dusk's legacy shapes everything

Unified only in 1887 from dozens of smaller kingdoms by Empress-General Amara Dusk — a Varak aura user who completed the unification campaign in eleven years and governed for another forty. Resource-rich beyond almost any other nation. The East African Rift ley line running through it combined with Vakrite deposits creates ambient mana conditions unique on earth.

Workers in the restricted interior zones around Varath have recently begun developing cultivation abilities without formal training. The Vaskaran military has classified their findings in that region. The Accord Watch's inquiry was answered with a document that said nothing.

*Notable for: Vakrite extraction — 70% of known world deposits. East African Rift ley line convergence. Kael Dusk's classified interior operations.*

---

### THE AZHARAN COMPACT
*Location: Middle East/Arabian Peninsula equivalent*
**Capital: AZHARAN PRIME** (Arabian Peninsula — junction of Arabian, Eurasian, and African plates, plus dominant maritime trade routes)
**Government:** Trade and defence compact of seven city-states

The wealthiest region per capita on earth — built on controlling the most trafficked maritime trade routes and a unique spatial magic tradition that developed from their ancient navigational culture. Azharan spatial mages are the best in the world at what they do. Decision-making requires consensus between seven city-states, making them slow but collectively formidable.

*Notable for: Spatial magic tradition. Richest magical equipment markets in the world. Veilspun Silk processing knowledge (exclusive).*

---

## DYNARI LANDMASS (Asia Equivalent)

### XAN
*Location: China equivalent*
**Capital:** Beijing equivalent (IRL name retained)
**Government:** The High Seat — same dynasty for four hundred years, nominally elected

The largest nation on earth by population. Empire in everything but name. Their theoretical framework for circle formation is centuries ahead of the western world. They don't publish it. The strongest magical nation on earth. At least three documented six-circle mages in active military service — more than most continents combined.

The Jade Meridian School — most prestigious magical institution on earth — accepts approximately forty foreign students per decade and is located in Vethara, not Xan's administrative capital, specifically because of the Himalayan fault line mana quality.

*Notable for: Jade Meridian School at Vethara. Most powerful magical nation. Four centuries of unbroken dynastic control.*

---

### DORAI
*Location: Japan/Korea equivalent*
**Capital: RYUKEN** (Tokyo area — junction of Philippine Plate, Pacific Plate, and Eurasian Plate. Most mana-dense city on earth.)
**Government:** Constitutional monarchy with genuinely powerful parliament

The counterweight to Xan. Smaller, more concentrated, arguably more refined in aura tradition specifically. The Ironblood Brotherhood was founded here and its spiritual centre — the Proving Grounds — remains in Dorai's highland interior, directly above one of the three plate boundaries beneath Ryuken.

Produces the highest average tier aura users per capita on earth. Consistently. The ambient mana density from the Ring of Fire fault convergences beneath Ryuken is the primary geological explanation.

Cold, formal relationship with Xan. Has almost gone to war twice in living memory. Hasn't.

*Notable for: Ironblood Brotherhood Proving Grounds. Ring of Fire mana density. Most mana-dense urban environment on earth (Ryuken).*

---

### INDARA
*Location: India/Southeast Asia equivalent*
**Capital:** Delhi equivalent (IRL name retained)
**Government:** Federal republic of thirty-two states

Ancient, vast, philosophically the most interesting nation on earth. Six distinct magical schools developed independently within its borders over two thousand years and all six still exist and argue. Currently the fastest growing economy on earth. The tension between ancient cultivation traditions and aggressive modernisation creates genuine cultural and political instability in interior states.

*Notable for: The Sixfold Debate — three hundred year ongoing academic and occasionally physical conflict between six independent schools of magical theory. Home to major Saal Faith populations and significant Leywater sources.*

---

### VETHARA
*(City, not nation — within Xan's territory near the Indaran border)*
*IRL Reference: Kathmandu/Lhasa area — Indian Plate driving under Eurasian Plate*

The most prestigious cultivation destination on earth for serious mage development. The Himalayan fault system produces deep, stable ley lines better suited to advanced circle formation than almost anywhere else — cultivators describe the mana here as heavy and old rather than bright and surface-level. Pre-Circle Wars structures still stand in the old district. Their material composition doesn't match any current geological survey.

*Notable for: Jade Meridian School. Deepest known ley line structures on earth. Oldest continuously inhabited city.*

---

## WESTMARCH (Americas Equivalent)

### ARNOVA
*Location: USA/Canada equivalent*
**Capital:** Washington equivalent (IRL name retained)
**Government:** Federal republic — genuinely the most independent nation from Order influence in the developed world

Young by world standards — formally unified 280 years ago from territories that broke from Valdris. Cultivation culture is chaotic by old world standards: no single dominant tradition, every immigrant wave brought every school of thought. Produces genuine innovation and genuine instability in roughly equal measure. The Aureate Compact has a presence here it finds difficult to leverage.

*Notable for: Multiple intact indigenous tribal cultivation traditions outside the mainstream framework. Studied, romanticised, frequently exploited.*

---

### CALDURA
*Location: Central/South America equivalent*
**Capital:** Varies by sub-nation (Caldura Norte, Esmerine Republic, Valdura)
**Government:** Patchwork — three dominant nations and dozens of smaller ones

The region sits on some of the most mana-dense geography on earth. The deep jungle interiors have measurably higher ambient mana than almost anywhere else, producing unusual cultivation phenomena in long-term inhabitants. Ancient civilisations here developed sophisticated cultivation traditions independently before contact with the old world. Most of that knowledge was systematically destroyed during a colonial period that Valdris and Sorenne remain diplomatically uncomfortable about.

**The Verdant Anomaly** — a region of jungle interior where ambient mana density is so extreme that uncultivated individuals sometimes spontaneously begin forming meridian connections. The Anomaly has been expanding. Its eastern boundary shifted outward approximately three kilometres in the past two years. Two bordering governments have agreed not to make this public.

*Notable for: The Verdant Anomaly. Pre-contact cultivation archive rumours. The highest mana-density interior geography on earth.*

---

## OCEANIA EQUIVALENT

### THALARA
*Location: Australia/Pacific equivalent*
**Capital:** Sydney equivalent (IRL name retained)
**Government:** Federation — politically neutral by necessity, indispensable by expertise

Punches far above its weight in one specific area: meridian medicine. The best medical system on earth for cultivation-related injuries, blockages, and rare physique complications developed here — partly from necessity (isolated population) and partly from a unique indigenous tradition of meridian reading that predates formal cultivation theory by centuries.

The indigenous tradition here never developed offensive cultivation. It developed diagnostic and healing applications. A Thalaran meridian reader can identify blockages, anomalies, and rare physique markers that formal Sorath medical theory still cannot reliably detect.

*Notable for: Thalaran Meridian Institute — the best cultivation medicine facility on earth. Years-long waiting lists. Heads of state have jumped the queue.*

---

## ANTARCTICA

### THE CONCLAVE OF VEIL
*Not a nation. Doesn't apply for recognition. Doesn't respond to diplomatic contact.*

Approximately two to three hundred years ago, a group of high-tier mages withdrew from world politics entirely and established a permanent settlement in the Antarctic interior. The most credible theory: they hit a theoretical ceiling in conventional circle formation and believed the interference of ambient mana from the world's population was limiting their ability to study pure mana behaviour. Antarctica's near-zero ambient mana density makes it paradoxically ideal for this.

They are known to exist. Satellite imagery confirms structures. No nation has successfully made contact in living memory. Two expeditions attempted to reach them in the last century — one turned back, one disappeared.

What they know, what they've achieved, and whether they are still pursuing magic theory or something else entirely is unknown.

*Notable for: The most unsettling blank space on the map. Every major Order and government has a file. None of the files say much.*

# PART FOUR: THE MAJOR ORDERS

---

## THE AUREATE COMPACT
**Founded:** ~950, Valdris
**Headquarters:** Auros Hall, Dunevorn, Valdris
**Focus:** Magic — circle formation, mana theory, certification, international politics
**Membership:** ~40,000 active members. ~100,000+ affiliates and alumni.
**Leader:** Grand Arbiter **SELAN VOSS**, 67, six circles

**On paper:** Certify mage rankings, maintain magical safety standards, mediate cultivator disputes, fund research, operate schools.

**In practice:** The closest thing the western world has to a shadow government for cultivators. They decide whose magical credentials are internationally recognised. They decide which noble houses receive backing. They have members embedded in thirty national governments. Their funding comes with strings not written in any contract.

They are not cartoonishly evil. Most members genuinely believe in their mission of order and stability. The problem is that their definition of stability aligns suspiciously well with whoever is currently powerful.

**Internal tension:** A reformist faction has been growing for twenty years — younger members who believe the Compact has become too politically entangled. The old guard considers them naive at best. This schism hasn't broken into open conflict yet.

**How they treat outsiders:** Politely. Carefully. They will help you if it costs them nothing or gains them something. They are never openly hostile unless you've made yourself their enemy, at which point you'll know it by the absence of doors opening rather than any direct confrontation.

---

## THE IRONBLOOD BROTHERHOOD
**Founded:** ~1100, Dorai highlands
**Headquarters:** Spiritual — Proving Grounds, Dorai interior. Administrative — Ostermark.
**Focus:** Aura — physical cultivation, combat doctrine, warrior tradition
**Membership:** Deliberately small. ~8,000 full members worldwide.
**Leader:** Grandmaster **TORVEN ASHK**, age disputed (believed 70s-90s)

**On paper:** Preserve and transmit aura cultivation tradition, operate the Proving Grounds, provide contracted security and military advisory services.

**In practice:** The most credible military force not attached to any nation state on earth. A Brotherhood contract is extraordinarily expensive and extraordinarily effective. Nations with Brotherhood advisors tend to win wars. Nations that have fought Brotherhood members directly tend to remember it.

They take contracts selectively. They have refused commissions from Ruthmark three times and Xan twice without explanation. They have never broken a contract once accepted.

**Internal tension:** Traditionalists (cultivation as pure personal mastery, Brotherhood exists to preserve that path) vs Pragmatists (Brotherhood survival requires political engagement and revenue). Current Grandmaster is a Traditionalist. Pragmatist faction is restless.

**How they treat outsiders:** Bluntly. If they respect you they'll tell you. If they don't they'll also tell you. The least corrupt major institution on Erith — too proud to be bought, too dangerous to be easily coerced.

---

## THE SAAL CONCLAVE
**Founded:** ~150 (oldest surviving institution on earth)
**Headquarters:** The Sanctum, Sorath capital
**Focus:** Magic — mana theory, meridian medicine, Saal philosophy
**Membership:** ~15,000 active. Concentrated in Karathi landmass.
**Leader:** First Voice **DARAN ESSE**, 54 (first First Voice in two centuries without founding Karathi bloodlines)

**On paper:** Preserve the Saal philosophical tradition, operate the Grand Archive, license meridian medical knowledge, train and certify cultivation medics.

**In practice:** Control the most comprehensive meridian and mana knowledge base on earth and monetise access ruthlessly. Every major hospital treating cultivation injuries pays Conclave licensing fees. The Grand Archive contains texts that could revolutionise magical theory worldwide. The Conclave decides who sees what and at what price.

**Internal tension:** Genuine theological split between members who see Saal philosophy as a spiritual path requiring devotion and those who are essentially secular academics who joined for Archive access. Additionally, First Voice Daran Esse is a genuine reformer who has proposed meaningful licensing reform four times. All four blocked by the Circle of Voices before reaching full vote.

A classified internal division called **Threshold Studies** has been acknowledged in three archived secondary records. All three files are now empty — contents removed after Esse's appointment, with no access log entry in the relevant period. The access log system was maintained by Elder Saan's administrative division.

**How they treat outsiders:** With serene, impeccable professionalism and a licensing fee.

---

## THE UNTETHERED
**Founded:** Unknown. First documented reference ~1200.
**Headquarters:** None confirmed.
**Focus:** The intersection of magic and aura — specifically whether a single cultivator can develop both systems simultaneously and what that would produce.
**Membership:** Unknown. Estimated hundreds to low thousands.

**What they actually do:** The conventional wisdom — taught in every magical institution — is that magic and aura are mutually exclusive development paths. Once meridians fully commit to one system, the other becomes inaccessible. The Untethered don't believe this. They believe mutual exclusivity is a limitation of incomplete development rather than fundamental law.

Whether they've produced a dual cultivator is unknown.

What is documented: they recruit specifically from people with rare physiques, clogged meridians, unusual meridian configurations, and people who don't fit existing cultivation frameworks. They view these individuals not as defective but as potentially closer to something the conventional system cannot produce.

They don't take contracts. They don't certify anyone. They don't appear to want money or political influence in any conventional sense.

**Internal structure:** Unknown.

**How they treat outsiders:** They approach people. People don't approach them. If an Untethered member contacts you, they've been watching you and decided you're interesting. Whether that's good depends entirely on why.

**Notable connections throughout history and current events:**
- References Verath the Grey by name in their one public statement without elaboration
- Acknowledges the Unknown God of the Ardent Pantheon without elaboration
- Has a name for the theoretical peak beyond Stage 9 that they don't share publicly
- Has a theoretical description of a physique they call the **First Vessel** — they have been searching for a confirmed case for sixty years
- Has a version of the twin myth that differs from every public version in at least one significant way — nobody outside knows what the difference is
- Believes that the mutual exclusivity of magic and aura is not fundamental law — that at the peak of either system, the distinction begins to blur
- Considers clogged meridians combined with a rare physique to be "the opposite of a disadvantage"

# PART FIVE: INTELLIGENT SPECIES

---

## HUMANS
The most numerous species on Erith. The baseline against which all cultivation systems were developed and are measured. No particular physiological advantages or disadvantages — their strength is adaptability and diversity. Cultivation innovation has historically come disproportionately from humans despite — or because of — their lack of species-wide specialisation.

---

## THE VAHARI (Elves)
**Population:** ~400 million worldwide
**Concentrated:** Dynari landmass — deep forest interiors and coastal regions. Significant diaspora in Valdris, Sorenne, Arnova.
**Lifespan:** 400-600 years naturally. Oldest documented: 847.

**Appearance:** Taller than average humans, lean build, distinctly elongated features and ears. Skin tones span full human range plus pale silver, deep gold, faint green-tinged. Eyes uniformly striking — larger irises, colours that don't occur in humans. Move with economy of motion humans often read as arrogance.

**Cultivation:** Longer, more numerous meridians than humans — natural advantage in circle formation. A Vahari with moderate talent develops circles faster than a comparably talented human. However their meridians are more rigid, less adaptable. Human cultivators develop unusual or hybrid techniques more readily. Vahari technique is refined, deep, and often inflexible. Genuine magical theory innovation has historically come disproportionately from humans despite the Vahari structural advantage.

**Society:** No single nation. The closest thing is **Vahareth** — a semi-autonomous region within Xan's borders, negotiated four centuries ago. Significant diaspora populations throughout human nations, fully integrated in Valdris and Sorenne, tracked in Ruthmark. Several sit on the Aureate Compact Council.

**The long view problem:** A Vahari who is 200 years old has watched human institutions rise and fall. Human politics — moving in election cycles and news weeks — feels genuinely incomprehensible to older Vahari. Younger Vahari growing up integrated into human society often find themselves caught between two timescales and belonging to neither.

## THE DUREN (Dwarves)
**Population:** ~180 million
**Concentrated:** Mountain ranges across Veltric and Central Asian landmass. Major urban populations in Ostermark. Significant communities in the Azharan Compact.
**Lifespan:** 250-350 years.

**Appearance:** Average height 150-160cm. Significantly heavier than a human of the same height — genuinely higher bone and muscle density, a direct physiological product of underground origin. Wide faces, strong jaw structure, hair grows unusually fast and thick. Skin tones toward deeper tones with a slight stony undertone — greys, deep browns, occasional faint reddish cast in northern populations.

**Cultivation:** Shorter, denser meridians — slower circle formation, most Duren never develop beyond two or three circles. What they have instead is exceptional aura cultivation capacity. Their bodies can withstand higher aura saturation before strain. A Duren aura cultivator at equivalent tier hits harder, endures more, recovers faster. The Ironblood Brotherhood has a disproportionate number of Duren members historically.

**Society:** Organised around **Holds** — extended family-clan structures controlling economic interests collectively. By 2020 the Duren dominate global banking and finance — not through conspiracy but through thinking generationally, having members who live 300 years and remember every deal, and having developed sophisticated contract and accounting systems centuries before human banking existed. The three largest private financial institutions on Erith are Duren-Hold controlled.

## THE VARAK (Orcs)
**Population:** ~300 million
**Concentrated:** Karathi landmass — sub-Saharan and horn of Africa equivalents. Significant populations in northern Ruthmark and scattered Americas communities.
**Lifespan:** 80-120 years. Mature faster — fully physically developed by 14.

**Appearance:** Large, powerfully built. Average height 185-200cm. Skin tones range from deep grey-green to dark brown to near black. Prominent lower canines visible when speaking or smiling. Heavy brow ridge. Facial expressions broad and readable — Varak are not stoic by nature.

**Cultivation:** The most naturally mana-conductive physiology for aura cultivation. Meridians are wider, bodies generate physical energy at higher baseline. The upper tiers of physical cultivation are disproportionately Varak historically. Magic is harder — same wide meridians that make aura flow easily make fine mana control difficult. Some Varak mages exist and are respected precisely for how hard it is. None documented above four circles.

**Society:** Historically tribal and clan-based. By 2020 Vaskara is the primary Varak nation — Empress-General Amara Dusk was Varak and her legacy shapes national identity. Face the most overt discrimination of any intelligent species in the western world — subtle in law, visible in practice. The Aureate Compact has had exactly one Varak Council member in its history. He resigned citing institutional obstruction. His resignation letter is a foundational document of Varak civil rights movements worldwide.

## THE KELDI (Gnomes)
**Population:** ~120 million
**Concentrated:** Ostermark and surrounding Veltric regions. Small but significant communities in the Azharan Compact and Arnova.
**Lifespan:** 180-220 years.

**Appearance:** Average height 120-130cm. Slight but wiry. Large eyes relative to face size, slightly oversized hands. Hair tends toward unusual natural colours — deep reds, bright whites, occasional natural blues in older populations (unexplained). Move quickly with a restless energy humans often find exhausting.

**Cultivation:** The most intricate meridian network of any species — densely networked, highly sensitive, capable of extremely fine mana manipulation. Natural innovators in magical theory — their structure allows perception and manipulation at a granular level others can't easily access. Weakness is capacity — smaller bodies mean lower total mana storage. Aura cultivation genuinely difficult — their physiology doesn't generate physical energy at the rate larger species do.

**Society:** Organised around **guilds** rather than family structures — professional organisations handling everything from trade to dispute resolution to social support. Guild membership is chosen not inherited — highly meritocratic internally, highly competitive. The dominant force in magitech development by 2020. The Ostermark magitech industry is essentially a Keldi-human collaborative enterprise where the Keldi provide disproportionate intellectual input and frequently complain about disproportionately little credit.

# PART SIX: BEASTS OF ERITH

---

## MANA CRYSTAL CORES — THE SHARD SYSTEM

All beasts of meaningful cultivation exposure develop a **mana crystal core** — a crystallised concentration of the mana they have absorbed over their lifetime. These are called **Shards** colloquially and are graded by the Saal Conclave (who charge licensing fees to use their grading system commercially).

**Grade 1 — Grey Shard:** Common beasts. Low density. Used in basic magitech, cheap cultivation aids, industrial applications. Worth little individually, valuable in bulk.

**Grade 2 — White Shard:** Uncommon beasts. Moderate density. Mid-tier magitech, medical applications, cultivation tools.

**Grade 3 — Blue Shard:** Strong regional beasts. High density. Advanced magitech, high-end equipment, cultivation medicine. Significantly valuable.

**Grade 4 — Gold Shard:** Apex regional predators. Very high density with elemental properties specific to the creature. Rare and expensive.

**Grade 5 — Crimson Shard:** Legendary creatures only. Extraordinarily rare. Properties vary wildly. Worth more than most people see in a lifetime.

**Shape and size matter within grades.** Specialists — appraisers, experienced hunters, certain Conclave scholars — can read a shard like a fingerprint: species, age, health, elemental affinity. Size is not always proportional to grade. A Crimson Shard from a legendary creature might be surprisingly small but luminous with density. A large Grey Shard from a massive mundane beast is still just a Grey Shard.

**The black market for cores** exists largely to feed the Corestone crafting industry, which operates partially outside Saal Conclave licensing because the margins justify the risk.

---

## TIER 1 — COMMON BEASTS
*Grey Shards. Found worldwide. The everyday dangerous wildlife of Erith.*

**MANAWOLVES** — The most widespread Tier 1 beast. Found on every continent except Antarctica. Essentially wolves enhanced by ambient mana absorption — larger, smarter, more tactically aware than mundane counterparts. Pack hunters. The mana absorption has pushed their instincts toward something approaching tactical thinking — they test defences before committing, pull back when a target proves too strong. A lone manawolf is manageable for a trained fighter. A pack of eight is dangerous for a low-tier cultivator. *Shard: Small, pale grey, slightly cloudy.*

**STONEBACK** — Heavily built boar-like creature found across Veltric and Karathi landmasses. Named for the ridge of dense, mana-hardened hide along its spine that resists blades. Solitary, aggressive when threatened, extremely durable for Tier 1. Farmers despise them. Their stoneback ridge is commercially harvested for low-end armour components and construction reinforcement. *Shard: Small, grey, angular.*

**DUSKWING** — Large predatory birds, Veltric and Karathi forest interiors. Wingspan 2-3 metres. Mana absorption has pushed sensory capabilities far beyond normal raptors — they can perceive mana flow, meaning they hunt cultivators as readily as mundane prey and can track a mana-using individual through dense forest. Rural cultivators in forests learn to suppress their mana signature or deal with Duskwings regularly. *Shard: Small, dark, slightly iridescent.*

**MIREBACK** — Aquatic, rivers and lakes across Dynari and Veltric landmasses. Resembles a large pike with bioluminescent patches pulsing when channelling mana. The channelling produces a localised electrical discharge in water — Mireback hunting is visible at night as pulses of cold blue light beneath the surface. Extremely dangerous to anyone in or near water. *Shard: Small, almost perfectly spherical — unusual regularity. Slightly more valuable than average Grey Shards for decorative magitech.*

**ASHCRAWLER** — Found in volcanic and geothermally active regions, predominantly Dynari mountain ranges and Karathi interior. Insectoid, roughly large-dog sized. Carapace has absorbed heat-aspected mana — genuinely thermally resistant. Bite delivers localised thermal discharge causing severe burns. Primarily scavengers — problem is their nests, which hold hundreds of individuals. A disturbed Ashcrawler nest is a genuine emergency. *Shard: Flat, disc-shaped, warm to the touch even after removal.*

**HOLLOW STAG** — Found in old-growth forests, Veltric landmass and Dynari interior. Resembles a large deer but with partially hollow bone structure filled with crystallised mana deposits, making them faintly luminous through the skin at joints. Passive unless threatened. A herd raises local ambient mana density measurably — they are walking mana concentrators. Their habitat is consequently claimed by cultivator schools and noble houses. Poaching technically illegal in most Veltric nations — enforcement inconsistent. *Shard: Elongated, slightly translucent, faint inner luminescence that persists after removal.*

## TIER 2 — UNCOMMON/REGIONAL BEASTS
*White and Blue Shards. Specific to certain regions. Require cultivator-level response.*

**IRONMANE LION** *(Vaskaran savannah)* — Significantly larger than ordinary lions (~280kg males) with a mane hardened into near-metallic filaments by iron-aspected mana. The mane is genuinely blade-resistant. Individually dangerous to a low-tier cultivator, seriously dangerous in a pride. Vaskaran and Varak hunting culture has deep traditions around Ironmane Lions — solo kills are a rite of passage in some communities. Mane filaments processed into flexible metal-weave armour components. *Shard: Dense, Blue grade, faint metallic sheen.*

**DEEPCALLER** *(Deep ocean, all major coastlines)* — Massive deep-water creature, morphology between whale and cephalopod. Adults reach thirty metres. Rarely seen due to depth preference. The danger is their mana emission — low frequency pulses that affect the nervous system of anything within range, causing disorientation, hallucination, and sustained-exposure unconsciousness. A beached Deepcaller is treated as a natural disaster. The passive mana emission from even a dying individual affects everyone within several hundred metres. *Shard: Blue grade, enormous, deep blue-black. Rarely recovered.*

**FROSTFANG** *(Northern tundra — Ruthmark, northern Arnova)* — Large predatory cat, sabre-tooth in build, cold-aspected. Body temperature measurably below ambient. Bite delivers localised freezing in tissue. Unusual for a non-intelligent creature: their cold mana absorption makes them nearly invisible to mana-sensing — they effectively suppress their signature. Patient hunters that stalk for hours. Disproportionately dangerous to cultivators who rely on mana-perception for threat detection. Ruthmark military has studied this sensory suppression extensively. *Shard: Pale, almost white, genuinely cold to the touch indefinitely after removal.*

**CINDERBACK TORTOISE** *(Volcanic regions — Dynari mountains, Caldura)* — Enormous (up to four metres shell length), slow, almost impossible to kill conventionally. Shell absorbed fire-aspected mana — cutting tools overheat rapidly on contact, shell radiates enough heat to cause burns from proximity. Not aggressive. Eats volcanic mineral deposits and ambient mana. The danger: a startled Cinderback discharges a thermal mana pulse incinerating everything within five metres. Legally protected in most nations that have them — their shells rapidly lose properties at death, making them worth more alive. Some Dynari communities have centuries-long coexistence traditions with specific individuals. *Shard: Gold grade, deep orange-red, genuinely hot, retains thermal charge for years.*

**VEILSTALKER** *(Deep forests worldwide — most common in Caldura and Dynari interiors)* — The most unsettling Tier 2 creature. Roughly humanoid in silhouette — bipedal, approximately two metres tall, long-limbed. Not intelligent. But the mana absorption has produced a spatial distortion effect around its body that bends light — genuinely difficult to look at directly. Not invisible — the eye refuses to focus. Peripheral vision catches it more clearly than direct sight. Hunts by following prey patiently over long distances before killing with physical strength. The spatial distortion appears defensive rather than active. *Shard: Blue grade, irregular, slightly refracts light in geometrically inconsistent ways. The Azharan Compact's spatial mage tradition has been studying recovered Veilstalker shards for decades. Connection to spatial magic theory significant and unresolved.*

## TIER 3 — LEGENDARY CREATURES
*Gold and Crimson Shards. Rare. Named. Treated as natural disasters.*

**WESTERN DRAGON** *(Veltric landmass — mountain ranges, deep wilderness)* — Apex predator of the European equivalent. Adults reach twenty metres body length, thirty-five to forty metre wingspan. Quadrupedal with wings. Elemental fire generation through direct mana-to-thermal conversion — burns hotter than conventional flame, cannot be extinguished by mundane means. Intelligent in the manner of a very old, very dangerous animal — not sapient, not communicative, but capable of problem-solving, recognising individuals, and learning from experience. Population: perhaps thirty to forty adults total on the Veltric landmass. Technically protected under Cultivator Accords as natural heritage species. The last documented adult kill was in 1987 — required eleven cultivators, two of whom died. The recovered Crimson Shard (torso-sized, deep red) is in a secure Compact facility. *Shard: Crimson grade. Torso-sized for large adults. Deep red, extraordinarily valuable.*

**THUNDERBIRD** *(Arnova interior and northern regions)* — The dominant legendary creature of the western hemisphere. Enormous avian — wingspan up to fifty metres in largest documented individuals. Lightning-aspected. A Thunderbird in flight produces localised storm conditions — cloud formation, high winds, directed lightning discharge. More territorial than aggressive — they don't specifically hunt humans. The danger is proximity: passive atmospheric effects are dangerous to anyone within several kilometres, and a disturbed Thunderbird produces non-discriminating electrical discharges. Population unknown — they range over enormous territories. The indigenous tribal communities of the Arnova interior have the most sophisticated understanding of Thunderbird behaviour on earth, accumulated over thousands of years. The mainstream academic establishment has been trying to formally access this knowledge for decades with limited success. *Shard: Gold grade, large, bright yellow. Crackles with residual static electricity indefinitely after removal.*

**THE ABYSSAL** *(Deep ocean, all major oceans)* — Not a single species — a classification for several distinct deep ocean creatures sharing one characteristic: they are so large and so mana-saturated that they affect ocean currents and ambient mana density across hundreds of kilometres. The largest documented encounter (1934, near Azharan coastline) involved something estimated at over two hundred metres. It surfaced for forty minutes. The mana disruption affected cultivation practice across the region for three weeks. Whether these are multiple species, one species with dramatic variation, or something else is unknown. No one who has studied one up close has published findings. *Shard: No confirmed recovery. Several unconfirmed ones in private collections.*

**THE PALE ONES** *(Antarctica adjacent — rare sightings in southern ocean)* — Possibly connected to the Conclave of Veil. Possibly not. Large, pale, vaguely cetacean in shape — but details of every documented sighting differ enough that it's unclear if witnesses see the same species each time. They don't attack ships. They follow them. Sometimes for hours. Sometimes for days. The feeling reported universally: of being examined rather than hunted. No recovered shard. No confirmed kills. Two research expeditions returned with equipment failures and crew members who declined to discuss what they saw.

# PART SEVEN: MATERIALS AND RESOURCES

---

## KNOWN AND TRADED

**VAKRITE** *(Geological — Vaskaran landmass primarily, trace deposits elsewhere)*
A dark, near-black mineral with a faint internal luminescence pulsing slowly like a heartbeat. Raises ambient mana density in its environment when raw. Applications: cultivation training facilities built around raw Vakrite produce measurably faster student development; medical facilities use it to accelerate meridian healing; high-end magitech uses processed Vakrite as a mana amplifier. Vaskara controls ~70% of known deposits. Every major power has economic, diplomatic, or covert interests in Vaskaran Vakrite extraction. High purity Vakrite is worth more per kilogram than gold.

**SOLVITE** *(Geological — crystalline deposits along major ley lines. Most concentrated: Dynari mountain ranges, Azharan desert interior)*
A translucent crystal ranging from pale blue to deep violet by purity. The most mana-conductive solid material known — transmits mana through its structure the way copper transmits electricity, with minimal loss over distance. Without Solvite, magitech as an industry does not exist. Processed into filaments for circuitry, ground to powder for inscription, used raw in high-end cultivation tools. The world's silicon equivalent — except rarer and geographically concentrated. The Dynari landmass controls the largest deposits.

**ASHSTONE** *(Geological — volcanic regions. Dynari mountain ranges, Caldura, scattered Karathi deposits)*
Dense, matte grey-black stone formed in mana-rich magma chambers. Extraordinary thermal resistance — doesn't conduct heat and doesn't degrade under temperatures that destroy conventional refractory materials. Used in high-temperature industrial facilities, cultivation training environments involving fire-aspected techniques, magitech components generating significant heat. The Cinderback Tortoise shell shares similar properties — Ashstone is the geological equivalent.

**MIREWOOD** *(Biological — old-growth forests with high ambient mana. Caldura deep jungle, Dynari forest interiors, scattered Veltric old-growth)*
Not a single species but a classification — trees grown in high mana environments long enough to absorb significant mana into their cellular structure. The result is denser, decay-resistant, mana-conductive timber ideal for inscription work and cultivation tool crafting. Old-growth Mirewood centuries old produces timber so saturated it's classified alongside mineral resources in value. Heavily regulated in theory. Massively over-harvested in practice. Caldura Mirewood deforestation is an ongoing environmental and political crisis.

**VEILSPUN SILK** *(Biological — harvested from living Veilstalker spatial distortion membrane)*
Extraordinarily difficult to harvest — requires proximity to a living, undisturbed Veilstalker. The membrane degrades rapidly after death. One of the most expensive materials per gram on earth. The spatial distortion properties translate into fabric that partially disrupts mana-sensing of the wearer — not invisibility, but reduces clarity and range of mana-sense significantly. Intelligence agencies, assassins, and certain cultivators pay extraordinary prices. The Azharan Compact has exclusive processing knowledge and doesn't share it.

**CORESTONE** *(Biological — refined from Grade 4+ mana crystal cores via Saal Conclave-licensed alchemical process)*
A processed material, not naturally occurring. High-grade cores refined through specific alchemical treatment produce a dense, dark red material retaining the elemental properties of the source creature in stable, workable solid form. Used in high-end weapon and armour crafting. A blade with fire-aspected Corestone carries permanent thermal properties. Armour incorporating Ironmane Lion Corestone has genuine physical resistance enhancement. Extremely expensive. The black market in unregistered cores exists largely to feed the Corestone industry operating outside licensing because the margins justify the risk.

**LEYWATER** *(Geological/Environmental — underground water running along major ley lines for extended distances. Most significant: Indaran subcontinent, parts of Karathi landmass)*
Water infused with ley line mana over geological timescales. Almost visually indistinguishable from normal water — very faintly luminescent in complete darkness. Consumed regularly, measurably accelerates meridian development and healing. Natural Leywater sources treated as sacred in most cultures that have them — several Indaran religious sites are built around springs in continuous use for thousands of years. Synthetic Leywater can be produced but is measurably less effective than natural product.

## RUMOURED OR PARTIALLY KNOWN

**VOIDITE** *(Deep ocean trenches — no confirmed surface sample)*
Theoretical material proposed by Azharan spatial mage researchers based on anomalous mana readings from deep ocean survey equipment. Theorised to absorb mana completely without saturation, re-emission, or any detectable effect — a perfect mana absorber. If it exists as theorised, the applications are enormous: shielding, cultivation medicine for overload treatment, weapons design. Whether it actually exists is debated. The Compact funds deep ocean survey expeditions periodically. Several have not returned.

**VERDANT RESIN** *(Biological — theorised to exist within the Verdant Anomaly, Caldura)*
The Anomaly's mana density is so extreme that standard biological processes in its interior operate differently. In the deepest documented penetration (a 1967 Arnovar expedition that reached approximately twelve kilometres in before turning back), trees were found weeping a thick, dark resin that caused spontaneous low-level circle formation in two expedition members who came into contact with it. The expedition report is classified. Both affected members died within a year. Three subsequent expeditions attempting to reach the same depth have failed. No reliable sample exists.

## UNKNOWN — NOTED AS EXISTING

**Deep Crust Material — Unnamed**
Seismic survey anomalies beneath the Dynari mountain ranges and beneath the Vaskaran interior suggest a material at 15-40km depth that interacts with mana in ways that don't match any known geology. Nobody has reached it. Current drilling technology combined with cultivation engineering could theoretically access the shallower anomalies but no serious public attempt has been made.

**Lunar Material — Unnamed**
Whatever exists on the moon — whether a relic from ancient space travel, naturally occurring lunar geology, or something else — is entirely unknown. No sample exists on Erith. No confirmed expedition has reached it in recorded history.

**Other Spatial/Cosmic Materials — Unnamed**
The Azharan spatial mage tradition has theoretical frameworks suggesting materials with fundamentally different mana properties exist beyond Erith's atmosphere. Whether this is academically sound or philosophical speculation dressed as physics is debated.

# PART EIGHT: KEY CITIES

**DUNEVORN** — Valdris capital
*IRL reference: Edinburgh/Scottish Highlands area — Highland Boundary Fault and Southern Uplands Fault*
Sits at a ley line convergence point where two significant fault systems meet — higher ambient mana than anywhere else on the Veltric landmass. The original settlement was a cultivation monastery (~400 AD) that became a fortress, then a city, then a capital. Cold, grey, politically vicious. Aureate Compact's Auros Hall dominates the skyline. The most mana-dense city on earth by significant margin. Three tectonic plates meeting creates a ley line convergence of scale existing nowhere else. Walking into Ryuken for the first time with open meridians feels like stepping from a dim room into sunlight. City architecture follows ley line patterns rather than conventional urban planning logic. The Ironblood Brotherhood's Proving Grounds are in the highlands two hours away, directly above one of the three plate boundaries.

**VETHARA** — Himalayan gateway (within Xan's territory near Indaran border)
*IRL reference: Roughly Kathmandu/Lhasa area — Indian Plate driving under Eurasian Plate*
Where the mountains begin. The ley lines here are deep and old — not the surface brightness of Ryuken but something denser and more stable, running into the earth. Cultivators describe the mana here as heavy rather than bright — better suited to deep circle formation than rapid development. The Jade Meridian School is located here specifically for the fault line mana quality. Pre-Circle Wars structures still stand in the old district. *IRL reference: Southern Italy/Sicily area — African and Eurasian plate boundary*
Oldest continuously powerful city in the western world. Already significant when the Karathi Empire was at its height. Currently effective seat of the Medori mage family, who have controlled it for four centuries regardless of nominal governance. Beautiful, extraordinarily corrupt, ancient.

**AZHARAN PRIME** — Azharan Compact hub
*IRL reference: Arabian Peninsula — Arabian, Eurasian, and African plate junction, plus dominant maritime trade routes*
The intersection of physical trade route significance and ley line spatial mana created ideal conditions for the Azharan spatial magic tradition to emerge organically. The richest magical equipment markets on earth operate here.

# PART NINE: RELIGIONS

## THE ARDENT PANTHEON
**Distribution:** Predominantly Veltric landmass and parts of Karathi. Significant Arnova diaspora communities.
**Estimated followers:** ~1.2 billion — most widely practiced religion on Erith.
**Organised by:** The Ardent Church — hierarchical, central seat in Sorenne. Led by **Arch-Voice Sera Mol**, 67.

**Core belief:** The world was shaped by a pantheon of divine beings who remain present and engaged. Prayer, devotion, and ritual practice open channels between worshippers and patron deities, through which divine gifts flow. What scholars call cultivation, the Ardent faithful call **Blessing** — the tangible manifestation of a god's favour in a devoted follower's body.

The Aureate Compact's official position: Ardent practitioners are simply cultivating through ritual methodology without understanding the underlying mechanics. The Ardent Church's position: the Compact are spiritually blind academics who've mistaken the mechanism for the source. Neither side has conclusively won the argument. Some Ardent practitioners develop abilities that don't map cleanly onto standard cultivation theory. The Compact files these as anomalies.

**The Gods:**

- **ARDEN** — God of War and Strength. Patriarch. Followers develop aura cultivation naturally through devotion. Temples double as training grounds. Clergy are warriors first.
- **SELA** — Goddess of Wisdom and Magic. Patron of scholars and mages. Followers develop magical circle formation with unusual theoretical intuition. Her temples are libraries.
- **MAREN** — God of the Sea and Travel. Patron of sailors and merchants. Followers develop spatial and wind-aspected cultivation naturally.
- **VETH** — Goddess of Death and Transition. Not malevolent — death in the Ardent tradition is a passage. Followers develop unusual sensitivity to death-aspected mana. The line between Veth worship and the Ashen Covenant is uncomfortably thin. Officially part of the pantheon. Quietly discouraged in most communities.
- **KAEL** — God of Fire and Forge. Patron of craftsmen, engineers, inventors. Followers develop fire-aspected cultivation and intuitive understanding of materials. Heavily worshipped in Ostermark's manufacturing culture.
- **LYRA** — Goddess of Life and Healing. Patron of medics and farmers. Followers develop meridian healing abilities that outpace what conventional medical training produces. Connection to Thalaran indigenous healing tradition academically significant.
- **THERON** — God of Lightning and Storms. Patron of cultivators who seek power above refinement. His followers are volatile, powerful, and disproportionately likely to experience meridian overload. Widely worshipped, quietly watched by cultivation authorities.
- **ORA** — Goddess of Shadows and Secrets. Patron of spies and those who operate outside the light. Officially recognised — removing her would fracture the faith. Her followers develop mana suppression and sensory cultivation naturally. Several intelligence agencies have Ora-devoted operatives.
- **VORAN** — God of Earth and Endurance. Patron of miners, builders, the patient. Heavily worshipped in Duren communities — so integrated into Hold culture that it's difficult to separate religious practice from Duren cultural identity.
- **THE UNKNOWN GOD** — No name. No temple. No clergy. Every version of the Ardent pantheon includes a tenth deity with no name, no domain, no doctrine — an acknowledgement that the pantheon is incomplete. Some theologians argue this is humility. Some argue it's a placeholder for something forgotten. The Untethered have referenced the Unknown God in their one public statement. Without elaboration.

## THE SAAL FAITH
**Distribution:** Predominantly Karathi landmass. Significant Indaran and Azharan communities.
**Estimated followers:** ~800 million.
**Organised by:** Loosely. The Saal Conclave is the academic branch. The Saal Faith as a living religion is decentralised — local communities, prayer, meditation, philosophical study. The two share roots and occasionally argue loudly about which more authentically represents the tradition.

**Core belief:** Mana is not a tool or a gift — it is the fundamental substance of existence. Everything that exists is a configuration of mana. The goal of existence is not power or salvation but **comprehension** — understanding one's own nature as a configuration of mana and through that understanding, harmony with everything else.

Cultivation in the Saal tradition is explicitly spiritual — not building power but deepening comprehension. Death is a reconfiguration — the mana constituting a person disperses and becomes part of something else.

---

## THE ASHEN COVENANT
**Distribution:** No official presence anywhere. Underground. Believed concentrated in certain Ruthmark border regions and the Caldura interior.
**Estimated followers:** Unknown. Deliberately obfuscated.
**Organised by:** Cell structure — small independent groups with limited knowledge of other cells. Intentionally resistant to infiltration.

**Core belief:** The twin myth got one thing right and one thing profoundly wrong. Life-aspected mana is half the equation. **Death-aspected mana** — released when living things die, when meridians break down, when the body returns to its base state — is equally real, equally potent, and deliberately suppressed by every major institution because those institutions are built on the living and fear what death-aspected cultivation implies.

When a person dies their meridian network collapses — that energy goes somewhere. The Covenant believes it can be harvested, shaped, and cultivated. Necromancy in this framework isn't raising the dead — it's capturing and wielding the energy death releases.

**The philosophical tension:** The rest of Erith hunts beasts, harvests their mana crystal cores, and uses that crystallised death-energy to power technology and cultivation. The Covenant simply applies this same logic beyond beasts. The line society draws is cultural not logical. A Covenant member with genuine philosophical consistency would note this. They would not be entirely wrong. This doesn't excuse the extremists who take the practice to atrocities. It does mean the persecuted moderate practitioners exist in genuine moral grey territory.

Veth of the Ardent Pantheon is quietly revered in some Covenant communities — considered the only honest member of a pantheon that pretends death is merely a transition rather than a power.

---

## THE UNMARKED
**Distribution:** Small communities worldwide. Most visible in Arnova where they have legal protection.
**Estimated followers:** ~50 million.
**Organised by:** Loosely congregational. No central authority. Local communities governed by elected elders.

**Core belief:** The twin myth was not a gift. It was a corruption. Humans were not meant to access meridian energy — the body's resistance to cultivation is the body knowing. Every circle formed, every stage of refinement, is a violation of the natural human state. The Unmarked do not cultivate and consider this a purity not a limitation.

**The uncomfortable question they keep asking:** What is the long-term effect of widespread meridian cultivation on the human species over centuries? Nobody with resources to answer it has tried.

**Statistical anomaly:** The Unmarked have a disproportionately high rate of rare physique births in their communities. They consider this irrelevant. Researchers consider it fascinating. The Untethered consider it significant.

## INDIGENOUS SPIRITUAL TRADITIONS
Not a unified religion. A category covering dozens of distinct traditions — Americas tribal communities, Thalaran indigenous practice, and scattered others — that developed cultivation understanding through spiritual rather than academic frameworks.

**Common threads across geographically separated traditions:**
- Cultivation as *relationship* rather than development — not building power but entering relationship with mana in the world
- Ancestral connection — deceased ancestors remain present as mana configurations and can be communicated with and drawn from
- Place as sacred — significant locations map exactly onto high ambient mana concentrations
- No separation between healing and combat cultivation — the same practice serves both purposes

These traditions are studied, romanticised, and persistently underestimated by mainstream cultivation academia. Thalaran meridian readers who have examined long-practising indigenous cultivators note unusual meridian configurations they can't fully explain using standard frameworks.

# PART TEN: TECHNOLOGY AND DAILY LIFE

---

## POWER AND ENERGY

The world runs on **Spell Lattice infrastructure** — Solvite-circuit devices with locked magical instruction sets fed by mana crystals. Fossil fuels exist and are used for industrial chemistry, materials manufacturing, and specific applications — but not for primary energy generation. The strategic energy resources are Vakrite and Solvite, not oil.

**How Spell Lattices work:** A device contains a Solvite circuit framework with a spell effect "locked" into its structure. Feed it a crystal, the crystal's mana activates the locked effect, produces the output, crystal depletes. Replace crystal. The device requires no cultivator to operate.

**Lattice grades by complexity:**
- **Grade 1:** Basic single-effect devices — heat, light, simple force. Powered by Grey/White Shards. Mass produced, cheap, everywhere. The average vehicle engine, household appliances, basic communications.
- **Grade 2:** More complex effects — compound outputs, sustained directional force, basic signal processing. Blue Shards for sustained operation. Industrial machinery, communications networks.
- **Grade 3:** Sophisticated multi-effect systems — medical devices, advanced computing, precision manufacturing. Gold Shards at peak. Requires specialist Keldi-level engineering.
- **Beyond Grade 3:** Currently impossible. The mana output required destroys any known containment material. Theoretical designs exist. No material currently available safely contains the operational stress. The race to solve containment materials is the most significant active research problem in magitech.

**The efficiency gap nobody discusses:** Crystal lattice power works but is almost certainly less energy-dense than alternatives never developed. The comparison to petroleum, and especially to nuclear energy, has never been formally calculated because the people who would fund that research benefit from the current system. The world is running on a suboptimal foundation entrenched by centuries of infrastructure investment. Whoever cracks an alternative energy source doesn't just have a better power supply — they have a fundamental strategic advantage the rest of the world's infrastructure cannot match without rebuilding.

---

## TRANSPORT

**Ground:** No internal combustion vehicles. Standard personal transport is a **magitech carriage** — wheeled vehicle with Grade 1-2 force and rotation lattice providing propulsion. Quiet, smooth, crystal-dependent. Magic animals widely used in rural areas, mountainous terrain, and regions where crystal supply chains are less reliable. Rail networks exist in developed nations using force lattice propulsion.

**Maritime:** The most developed transport sector. Centuries of maritime dominance before magitech meant ships came first and lattice propulsion was retrofitted. Modern magitech ships are fast and reliable. Azharan Compact ships are the finest — spatial magic-assisted navigation combined with Ostermark-equivalent lattice engineering.

**Air:** No meaningful aviation industry. Spatial teleportation handles long-distance rapid travel for those who can afford it. Everyone else takes ships. Some experimental Keldi lattice-lift platforms exist but never became a transport priority.

## COMMUNICATIONS

A functional global **communications network** equivalent to combined internet and telephone, running on signal transmission lattices through Solvite-fibre lines laid across continents and under oceans. Personal communication devices exist — crystal-powered, Solvite-circuit based, voice, text, and data capable. Broadcasting exists — audio and visual through lattice networks. State-controlled and private channels. Ruthmark's state broadcast is the most sophisticated propaganda apparatus on earth.

An encrypted equivalent of the dark web operates — used by Orders, intelligence agencies, the Ashen Covenant, criminal organisations, and anyone not wanting visible traffic.

## MILITARY TECHNOLOGY

The most divergent area from IRL.

**No firearms in wide circulation.** A trained cultivator outperforms any projectile weapon at close to mid range. Resources that would have gone into weapons development went into cultivation training instead.

**What exists:**
- **Conventional weapons:** Bladed weapons, polearms, bows. Still standard issue for non-cultivator military units. Reliable, no crystal supply chain required. Modern metallurgy applied to ancient forms — best military blades incorporate alloys and sometimes Corestone components unavailable in the medieval period.
- **Lattice weapons:** Handheld devices with offensive spell lattices. Force projection, thermal discharge, elemental effects. Limited by Grade 3 containment ceiling. Effective against non-cultivators and low-tier cultivators. Largely ineffective against Stage 5+ aura coating. Standard issue in cultivator-light environments.
- **Artillery equivalents:** Large fixed or vehicle-mounted lattice systems using massive Solvite housings to approach Grade 3+ output. Devastating against conventional forces and infrastructure. Still largely ineffective against high-tier cultivators.
- **Cultivator units:** The actual decisive military factor. Every major military has tiered cultivator units. Anything above Stage 7 or 6th circle is classified as a weapon of mass destruction under the Cultivator Accords and theoretically regulated. Practically every major power has them and everyone knows it.

## MEDICINE

**Cultivation medicine:** Centuries ahead of IRL in specific areas — meridian surgery, mana-based cellular repair, aura-assisted recovery. The Thalaran Meridian Institute represents the peak. Injuries that would be fatal in our world are treatable if you can access the right facility and afford it.

**Conventional medicine:** Roughly equivalent to our early-to-mid 20th century level. Germ theory exists and is understood. Antibiotics exist. Surgery is solid. Medical imaging is less advanced than IRL because lattice-based mana scanning does most of what MRI and CT do.

**Rare physique medicine:** The specialised field of identifying and managing unusual meridian configurations. Centred around the Saal Conclave's licensing framework and Thalaran diagnostic tradition. The most expensive medical specialty on earth.

---

## COMPUTING

Functional computing exists — Solvite-circuit based rather than silicon-based, performing equivalent functions. Somewhat behind IRL 2020 computing power — roughly equivalent to our mid-2000s to early-2010s level. The absence of the specific industrial pressures that created silicon semiconductor development means peak computing capability is lower.

The Keldi dominate computing development. Several Keldi guild consortiums are the equivalent of IRL's major tech companies.

No artificial intelligence equivalent in any meaningful form — the computing architecture optimised for lattice control and mana-signal processing rather than the specific mathematical operations that produced machine learning in our world.

## DAILY LIFE

**The ordinary person:** Roughly 60-70% of Erith's population has some degree of cultivation ability. Of those, maybe half have done anything meaningful with it. Of those, maybe a quarter have reached a level that practically matters. The average person on the street is someone who probably tried cultivation, got to Body Tempering or early Bone Refinement, decided it wasn't worth continued effort, and lives normally. They're slightly healthier than a pre-cultivation human baseline, heal a little faster, and have a passing understanding of meridians the way our world's average person understands car engines.

**Urban texture:** A major city in 2020 feels recognisably modern but distinctly different. Solvite power lines running into buildings, light lattice street fixtures, magitech carriages in traffic. Buildings are a mix of ancient stone and modern construction — in older cities, architectural layers are visible. Cultivation supply shops are as common as pharmacies — selling crystals by grade, basic lattice tools, cultivation aids, meridian health supplements. Training halls are neighbourhood institutions — combination of gym, dojo, and community centre, ranging from centuries-old prestigious institutions to street-level operations.

**Class and cultivation:** The most significant social dividing line isn't race or nationality — it's cultivation tier and access to training. High-tier cultivation requires time, resources, proper instruction, and often access to high ambient mana environments. All of these cost money or connections. A child born into a noble cultivation house with the Saal Conclave's best meridian medicine will develop faster and higher than an equally talented child born in a rural community with no instruction. The system isn't explicitly rigged — talent matters and exceptional individuals from ordinary backgrounds do break through — but the infrastructure heavily favours those who already have. The gap is visible and embodied in a way economic inequality alone isn't.

**The feeling of it:** Daily life in Erith 2020 feels like our world in texture and rhythm — recognisably modern — but with a constant background awareness that some people are operating on a fundamentally different physical level than others. A Stage 5 cultivator moves through space differently, occupies it differently. The air has a quality your body registers before your brain does. Most people have learned to background that awareness. In a quiet street at night when someone rounds the corner and your body knows before you consciously process it that this person is something beyond ordinary — that's Erith.

# PART ELEVEN: CULTIVATION SYSTEMS

---

## OVERVIEW

Both cultivation systems are rooted in the same biological infrastructure — **meridians** (channels running through the body) and **acupoints/nodes** (junction points along them). Both use Living Mana as their fuel. The systems differ in storage, refinement, application, and the fundamental architecture of how the body interfaces with mana.

**Meridian anomalies:**
- **Clogged:** Meridians partially or fully blocked at key nodes — prevents energy flow, cannot cultivate by conventional means. Affects roughly 1 in 200 people to some degree. Full blockages considered permanent by mainstream medicine. The Untethered do not consider them permanent.
- **Overloading:** Pulling in more natural energy than the body can safely handle. Dangerous. Can cause permanent meridian damage or death. Certain cultivation paths are more overload-prone than others.

---

## MAGIC — THE CIRCLE SYSTEM

Magic circles form at meridian nodes or branch off them as separate structures. They are physical constructs inside the body — reservoirs that the more circles you have, the deeper the total capacity. Forming each new circle requires the previous reservoir to be fully saturated, the meridians sufficiently conditioned to withstand the process, and increasingly — something harder to quantify. A will or comprehension that pure training doesn't guarantee. Many mages plateau permanently not from lack of power but from hitting a ceiling they can't identify or push through.

**Classification:**

| Circles | Classification | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1st Circle | Entry level | Basic mana applications. Useful but not dangerous. |
| 2nd-3rd Circle | Competent practitioner | Working mages in institutions, medical, magitech. Respected. |
| 4th-5th Circle | Serious mage | Noble house members, senior Order practitioners. Significant social weight. |
| 6th-7th Circle | Exceptional | Senior Compact Arbiters, heads of mage families. Strategic military assets. A 7th circle mage is a resource nations protect carefully. |
| 8th-9th Circle | Extraordinary | Known internationally by name. Tracked by intelligence agencies. Maybe 5-10 alive at any time. A conflict involving one is not a normal conflict — it is a crisis. |
| 10th Circle | Theoretical apex | One documented case in recorded history. Name lost or deliberately obscured. The Saal Conclave keeps accounts of their capabilities in restricted access. Whether anyone living could achieve it is unknown. |

---

## AURA — THE REFINEMENT STAGES

Where the mage system is clinical and numbered, the aura system is a physical journey with named stages. Different cultures use different terminology — the Dynari tradition, the Ironblood Brotherhood, Varak clan systems all have their own names. The academic standard terminology (from the Brotherhood's Ostermark administrative centre) is used here.

**Stage 1 — BODY TEMPERING**
Meridians begin drawing in ambient physical energy consistently. Macro-level changes — muscle density increases beyond conventional training, reaction time sharpens, endurance extends. A Body Tempering cultivator looks and moves like an exceptionally fit human. Nothing obviously supernatural yet.
*In a fight: Meaningfully stronger and faster than a non-cultivator. Still killable by conventional means.*

**Stage 2 — BONE REFINEMENT**
Physical energy penetrates deeper — skeletal structure densifies and hardens. Bones resist impact and fracture beyond normal. A Bone Refinement cultivator hit with a blade will still cut but the resistance is noticeable. Strikes land with weight that doesn't match visible size.
*In a fight: Requires a cultivator response or significant numerical advantage to handle safely.*

**Stage 3 — MARROW REFINEMENT**
The interior transforms. Cellular production system enhanced — faster healing baseline, recovery rate becomes visibly abnormal. Wounds that would take weeks to heal close in days. Eyes sometimes change slightly — faint luminescence under exertion, a sharpness that wasn't there before.
*In a fight: Serious threat to anyone without cultivation. Dangerous to low-tier cultivators. A genuine military asset.*

**Stage 4 — BLOOD REFINEMENT**
Physical energy circulates through the bloodstream. Faint aura traces visible along veins and skin surface during peak effort. Speed and strength increases become dramatic — non-cultivators cannot track a Stage 4 in serious combat. Last purely internal stage.
*In a fight: Dangerous to mid-tier cultivators. Can engage multiple lower stage opponents simultaneously.*

**Stage 5 — AURA AWAKENING**
First stage where aura becomes externally visible without deliberate effort. Faint field around the body. Pressure that cultivators can sense and sensitive non-cultivators can feel without understanding why. Other cultivators can now accurately read this person's stage at a glance — before this, assessment requires testing.
*In a fight: Small groups of mid-tier cultivators required. Conventional military forces need large numbers and specific preparation.*

**Stage 6 — AURA PROJECTION**
Aura projected beyond the body's surface as deliberate force — reinforcing strikes, extending effective range, disrupting others' cultivation with projected pressure. A weapon held by a Stage 6 cultivator isn't just a weapon — it's an extension of their refined body, aura carried through every strike. The Ironblood Brotherhood considers Stage 6 the first tier of genuine mastery.
*In a fight: High-tier cultivators required. Conventional forces essentially irrelevant regardless of numbers. A Stage 6 is a regional military factor.*

**Stage 7 — AURA FORMING**
Aura can be shaped into external constructs — weapons, barriers, armour extensions, concentrated projectiles. The cultivator is no longer limited to their physical body as a weapon. Stage 7 constructs begin affecting mana in the environment in ways pure physical force shouldn't. Not magic — but something that interacts with magic in ways lower stages don't. Scholars note it, debate it, don't have a satisfying explanation.
*In a fight: Mid-circle mages required to match. Nationally significant threat.*

**Stage 8 — AURA DOMAIN**
Aura extends into an area — a Domain. A cultivator's refined physical energy permeates the surrounding environment, suppressing weaker cultivators, altering physical properties of the space. The ground feels heavier. Movement is harder. The air has weight. Every Stage 8's Domain has its own character — shaped by their personal cultivation path, body's elemental affinities, their comprehension of what their aura fundamentally is. No two Domains are identical. Domain effects documented affecting magic circles directly — not counter-spelling but altering ambient conditions, which shouldn't be possible through physical means. The blurring is undeniable at this stage.
*In a fight: Only other Stage 8+ or high-circle mages represent realistic engagement.*

**Stage 9 — AURA EMBODIMENT**
The distinction between the cultivator's body and their aura begins dissolving. Not metaphorically — measurably. Stage 9 cultivators have survived injuries that destroyed tissues that should be vital because those tissues are no longer entirely biological. Movement has a quality observers describe not as speed but as the absence of the usual relationship between intention and position. Effects produced look genuinely magical to outside observers — not circle-based magic, something different. Something mage scholars find deeply uncomfortable.

Perhaps fifteen documented Stage 9 cultivators in recorded history. Several ancient figures who supposedly reached space are theorised to have been Stage 9. Whether anyone living has achieved it is unknown.
*In a fight: A Stage 9 is not a combatant. They are an event.*

**Stage 10 — UNNAMED**
Theoretical. No documented cases. Pre-Circle Wars texts reference something beyond Stage 9 in language that doesn't translate cleanly — words meaning simultaneously "completion," "return," and "origin." The Untethered have an internal name for it they don't share.

---

## THE CONVERGENCE — WHERE THE SYSTEMS BLUR

At the absolute peak of either cultivation path, something happens that mainstream theory cannot fully account for.

Stage 8 aura Domains are documented affecting magic circles directly. Stage 9 aura users produce effects that look magical to outside observers. High-circle mages at the 8th-9th level exhibit physical capabilities that should exceed their biological baseline.

The Untethered's position — never publicly confirmed but inferable from their actions and interests — is that this blurring is not coincidence. That mana and physical energy are expressions of the same underlying thing, and masters of either system eventually approach that truth from opposite directions without fully recognising what they're reaching.

The dual path — one person developing both systems — isn't about learning two separate things. It's about finding what they have in common at their root.

**The Dantian Path (theoretical — unknown to mainstream cultivation academia):**
A third theoretical architecture proposed by no known academic institution and held as classified research only within the Untethered. Instead of connecting meridians to circle anchors (magic) or to the heart (aura), a self-sustaining mana construct — a **Dantian** — is formed from mana itself and acts as the central node meridians feed into and draw from simultaneously. The Dantian is path-agnostic. Its output early on resembles magic or aura because those are the only reference points that exist. As it refines, the output becomes clearly something else entirely.

**Progression along three axes rather than a single scale:**
- **Density** — capacity, how much mana the Dantian can hold
- **Stability** — purity and refinement of the mana held
- **Flow** — efficiency with which mana routes through the meridians

**Formation conditions:** Conventional formation requires either a rare physique with unusual mana accumulation capacity, or the deliberate destruction of an existing cultivation system to release the required mana volume. The destruction path carries an interesting characteristic — it requires profound humility. A Stage 9 cultivator has spent their entire life building their power. Asking them to voluntarily destroy it for something uncertain requires a specific kind of self-abnegation that the cultivation path itself tends to erode. Lower-tier practitioners have less to lose and crucially, less ego invested — making them paradoxically better candidates for the destruction-path attempt than those with more power.

The Untethered have a theoretical description of a physique configuration optimal for Dantian formation. They call it the **First Vessel**. They have been searching for a confirmed case for sixty years.

---

## RARE PHYSIQUES

Beyond individual talent and effort, some people are born with meridian configurations that fundamentally alter cultivation potential. Formally classified by the Saal Conclave's meridian medicine tradition. Range from moderately unusual to world-alteringly rare.

### UNCOMMON PHYSIQUES
*~1 in 500 people. Noticeable advantage, not extraordinary.*

- **Deep Meridian** — Longer than average meridians, natural magic circle formation advantage. Common enough to be a known trait in certain bloodlines.
- **Dense Core** — Physical energy generation above baseline. Aura cultivation progresses faster in early stages.
- **Elemental Affinity** — Natural resonance with a specific element (fire, water, earth, wind, lightning). Magic or aura developed along that element measurably more potent.

### RARE PHYSIQUES
*~1 in 5,000 people. Significant. Noble houses track these in bloodlines.*

- **Twin Meridian** — Parallel meridian channels running alongside primary ones. Essentially double natural capacity. Development is harder — managing two channels is complex — but the ceiling is significantly higher.
- **Iron Physique** — Body density and natural aura resistance far above baseline. Bone and Marrow Refinement stages progress at extraordinary speed. Physical durability at every stage above what the tier would normally suggest.
- **Void Affinity** — Natural resonance with spatial and dimensional mana rather than elemental types. Exceptionally rare even among rare physiques. The Azharan Compact has been known to quietly acquire individuals with this trait.

### EXCEPTIONAL PHYSIQUES
*~1 in 50,000 people. Historically significant.*

- **Primordial Body** — Meridians seemingly predating standard human configuration. Wider, more numerous, connecting in patterns that don't appear in any standard anatomical map. Cultivation in any direction proceeds at a pace defying conventional understanding. The Saal Conclave has seven documented cases in their entire recorded history.
- **Convergence Physique** — Meridians structured in a way that doesn't naturally commit to either magic or aura pathways. Should be a disadvantage — natural development in either direction is slower initially. The Untethered believe it is the opposite of a disadvantage. No confirmed case of someone with this physique developing fully in either direction exists in public record.
- **Ethereal Marrow** — Meridians partially composed of crystallised Cosmic Mana deposits rather than purely biological tissue, allowing passive absorption of all three mana types simultaneously (Cosmic, Living, and Dying) rather than just Living Mana. The crystalline composition makes meridians unusually resilient but means standard cultivation techniques don't apply — standard assessment tools fail to identify it, returning "clogged" or "aberrant" results. Extremely rare. Possibly described in Solen Ess's unpublished 30%. The Saal Conclave has no confirmed documented case in their public records.

# PART TWELVE: THE CULTIVATOR ACCORDS

---

**Established:** 1923, following the conclusion of the world-war equivalent.
**Signatories:** 171 of 190 recognised nations.
**Enforcement Body:** The **Accord Watch** — one appointed member per signatory nation, headquartered in Valdris. Small investigative branch (~200 personnel) including a retainer of twelve high-tier cultivators used purely as assessors.

**What the Accords are:** The world's equivalent of the Geneva Convention, specifically governing cultivator conduct in warfare. Wartime framework only — domestic law governs civilian cultivation practice, varying significantly by nation.

---

**PROHIBITED — Outright banned in any conflict:**
- Techniques causing permanent meridian destruction in non-combatants
- Mass mana detonation — deliberate overloading of ambient mana in a populated area
- Cultivation-based environmental destruction above a defined threshold
- Using cultivation to contaminate water or food supplies
- Forced cultivation experiments on prisoners or civilians

**REGULATED — Permitted under specific declared conditions:**
- Stage 7+ aura or 6th circle+ mage deployment in declared conflicts requires advance notification to the Accord Watch
- Stage 8+ or 7th circle+ deployment requires formal request and acknowledgement — theoretically giving the Watch time to mediate before escalation
- Certain high-destruction techniques permitted only against military targets with documented post-hoc justification

**PROTECTED:**
- Cultivator medics under Accord Watch designation are non-combatants regardless of personal tier
- Captured cultivators cannot be subjected to forced meridian examination beyond necessary containment
- Civilian cultivators in occupied territories retain basic cultivation rights

---

**ENFORCEMENT AND CONSEQUENCES:**
The Accord Watch investigates reported violations, issues formal rulings, and recommends collective diplomatic responses — sanctions, asset freezes, World Assembly suspension. No military capacity. Power is entirely moral authority and collective political consequence.

In practice: smaller nations comply because the political cost of violation outweighs the benefit. Major powers comply with the letter while finding the gaps. Nobody has faced meaningful consequences for a regulated deployment violation because the nations with enough power to sanction a major violator are usually the ones doing the violating.

---

**THE GREY AREAS:**
- The Accords govern declared conflicts. Covert operations, proxy wars, and "counter-cultivation exercises" inside another nation's borders are technically outside scope.
- Private Order operations aren't covered — an Ironblood Brotherhood contract isn't a national military deployment.
- Tier thresholds for regulated deployment are self-reported. The Accord Watch has no independent verification means.
- "Civilian" vs "military cultivator" is defined by the nation involved, not the Watch.

The gaps are almost deliberate. Nobody who signed it wanted it to actually have teeth. They wanted to be seen signing it.

# PART FIFTEEN: GAME MECHANICS

---

## THE DICE SYSTEM

**Base:** D20 roll + relevant Stat Modifier compared against a Difficulty Rating set by the situation. Outcomes are always relative to DR — never fixed numbers.

**Outcome Tiers:**

| Roll vs DR | Outcome |
|---|---|
| 6+ above DR | Critical Success — exceeds expectations, bonus effect |
| 0-5 above DR | Success — intended result achieved cleanly |
| 1-4 below DR | Partial Success — achieved but with complication or cost |
| 5-9 below DR | Failure — doesn't work, consequences scale with situation |
| 10+ below DR | Critical Failure — fails badly, significant consequence |

**Difficulty Ratings:**

| Task | DR |
|---|---|
| Trivial | 4 |
| Easy | 7 |
| Moderate | 11 |
| Hard | 15 |
| Very Hard | 18 |
| Near Impossible | 22+ |

A DR above 20 isn't unclearable — a high enough stat modifier can still reach it. It requires genuine exceptional ability.

---

## CORE STATS

Six stats, each running 1-10 for ordinary humans. Cultivation and rare physiques can push beyond 10 over time.

| Stat | Governs |
|---|---|
| **Physique** | Raw strength, endurance, structural resilience, melee, withstanding physical punishment. Relevant to aura cultivation — higher Physique means the body can withstand higher aura saturation. |
| **Reflex** | Speed, agility, reaction time, precision. Dodging, quick strikes, fine motor actions, timing over raw power. |
| **Perception** | Environmental awareness, reading people, noticing details, sensing mana and cultivation presence. Spotting threats, detecting lies, reading a room. |
| **Intellect** | Reasoning, knowledge, problem-solving, magical theory, engineering. Understanding systems, recalling information, designing things. |
| **Willpower** | Mental fortitude, resisting external effects, pushing through limits. Resisting cultivation pressure from stronger opponents, maintaining focus under duress. |
| **Cultivation** | Tracks cultivation ability and current stage or circle count. Governs everything directly cultivation-related. For clogged characters: starts Locked. |

**Stat Modifiers:**

| Score | Modifier |
|---|---|
| 1-2 | -3 |
| 3-4 | -1 |
| 5-6 | +0 |
| 7-8 | +2 |
| 9-10 | +3 |
| 11-12 | +4 |
| 13+ | +5 |

---

## ABILITIES

**Passive:** Always active, no cost. Applied automatically when relevant.

**Active:** Require conscious use and cost something — stamina, mana, a condition toll, or a resource. High power abilities cost more.

Abilities are acquired through character background, cultivation progression, training during play, and discovery.

---

## HEALTH — CONDITION STATES

| State | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Fine** | No impairment. Operating normally. |
| **Strained** | Minor toll accumulating. No mechanical penalty but ignored long enough becomes Injured. Rest clears it. |
| **Injured** | -2 to Physique and Reflex rolls. Some physically demanding actions require a Willpower check to attempt. Needs rest and treatment to clear. |
| **Seriously Injured** | -3 to Physique and Reflex, -1 to all others. Certain actions unavailable without a critical Willpower success. Requires medical attention to stabilise. |
| **Critical** | All rolls at -4. Most physically demanding actions unavailable. Immediate treatment required or condition worsens passively. |
| **Incapacitated** | Cannot act meaningfully. |
| **Dead** | Possible. Requires reaching Critical and then a further significant damaging event without intervention. Not a single bad roll — a sequence of decisions and bad luck. |

## INVENTORY

Tracked practically. Items listed with condition where relevant — weapons degrade, crystals deplete, consumables are used. No rigid carrying limit but encumbrance applies common sense.

**Equipment Condition:** Pristine / Worn / Damaged / Broken

**Crystal Supply:** Tracked separately by grade. Depletes with use.

## CURRENCY

Tracked in universal **Marks** representing rough equivalent purchasing power.

| Amount | Gets You |
|---|---|
| 1-5 Marks | Basic meal, cheap supplies, Grade 1 crystals |
| 10-50 Marks | Quality equipment, comfortable lodging, Grade 2 crystals |
| 100-500 Marks | Significant purchase, minor magitech device |
| 1,000+ Marks | Major equipment, Grade 3+ crystals, significant favours |
| 10,000+ Marks | Life-changing sums, Grade 4 crystals, serious institutional attention |

---

## COMBAT

**Narrative first, rolls to adjudicate outcomes.** Describe what your character does. The GM describes environment, enemies, and consequences. Rolls happen at decision points where the outcome is genuinely uncertain and both success and failure are interesting.

**Initiative:** Reflex check at the start of contested situations. Sets who has the advantage of pace — not rigid turn order.

**Attacks:** Physique for physical strikes, Reflex for precise or speed-based ones, Cultivation for cultivation-powered attacks. Opposed by target's relevant defensive stat when actively defending.

**Damage:** Successful strikes move condition states depending on severity, target toughness, and weapon. A light hit on a healthy target produces Strained. A serious hit produces Injured. A devastating hit from a powerful cultivator can jump states.
